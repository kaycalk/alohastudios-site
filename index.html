Ongoing field work

Kīpuka — a document of wild Hawaiʻi.

A kīpuka is an island of old-growth forest spared by a lava flow — life holding its ground. This is our long-term project photographing the island's native forests, coastlines, and the conservation work protecting them.

About the studio

We traded the corporate ladder for the coastline.

Aloha Studio Productions is Nathan and Kayla — a two-person studio on the Island of Hawaiʻi, built on a bet that we'd do our best work in the place we most wanted to be.

Nathan spent years in enterprise tech helping the world's biggest companies tell stories with data. That discipline runs through every production: a real brief, a shot plan sequenced to the light, and work measured by whether it moves the numbers — bookings, conversions, sell-through.

Kayla is the eye in post. Every frame that leaves this studio passes through her edit — color, tone, and the restraint to know when an image is done. If our work has a signature, she's it.

In 2026 we left Houston, drove 3,000 miles of the American West with cameras rolling, and landed here. We shoot the way we chose this island — for the light, the texture, and the feeling you can't fake.

— Nathan & Kayla
